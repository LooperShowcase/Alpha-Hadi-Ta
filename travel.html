Top 3 Airlines in the world

1. Singapore Airlines

n 2005 AirAsia, a low-cost carrier (LCC) based in Malaysia, accused Singapore Airlines of double standards, when it claimed that the Government of Singapore attempted to keep it out of the Singapore market, despite desiring to fly routes out of Australia itself.[43] In 2007 Singapore Airlines welcomed[44][45] the liberalisation of the Singapore- Kuala Lumpur route, previously restricted to Singapore Airlines and Malaysia Airlines under rules designed to protect the state-run airlines from competition for over three decades, [46][47] accounting for about 85% of the over 200 flight frequencies then operated.[48] A highly lucrative route for LCCs due to its short distance and heavy traffic as the fourth-busiest in Asia,[49][50] bringing Singapore Airline's capacity share on the route down to about 46.7%, Malaysia Airlines' down to 25.3%, and increase to 17.3% to the three LCCs now permitted on the route, and the remainder shared by three other airlines as of 22 September 2008.[51] Until 1 December 2008, Singapore Airlines operated six flights per day. Singapore Airlines operated four flights per day plans from 1 December 2008 when the route was completely opened, while its sister airline SilkAir also operated four flights per day.[52] Malaysia Airlines, the main opponent to the liberalisation of the route[53] and deemed to be the party that stands to lose the most, continued to codeshare with both Singapore Airlines and SilkAir on the route.

2.Qantas

QF9/QF10 (Perth to London): Qantas operates a direct flight between Perth Airport (PER) in Western Australia and London Heathrow Airport (LHR) in the United Kingdom. This route is one of the longest non-stop flights in the world, covering a distance of approximately 9,009 miles. The flight takes around 17 hours and 20 minutes westbound and 18 hours and 10 minutes eastbound. Qantas operates this route using Boeing 787-9 Dreamliner aircraft, offering passengers a comfortable travel experience with features such as improved air quality and larger windows.

3. Emirates

EK215/EK216 (Los Angeles to Dubai): Emirates operates a popular long-haul flight between Los Angeles International Airport (LAX) in the United States and Dubai International Airport (DXB) in the United Arab Emirates. The flight covers a distance of approximately 8,339 miles and takes around 16 hours and 35 minutes westbound and 15 hours and 50 minutes eastbound. Emirates operates this route using Airbus A380 aircraft, known for their spacious cabins, in-flight entertainment options, and luxurious amenities in premium classes.
